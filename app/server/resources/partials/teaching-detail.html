Teaching FAQ's | Teach a course

How does Dactic work?

Developers use our site to get together and learn new things from other developers via affordable, semi-structured, in-person, mentored courses - like Coursera, except in-person.

It's about developers teaching developers. Courses are usually held on workday evenings, or weekends, and are attended by curious types who want to learn new things - typically professional developers.

We advertise these courses on our website, and have students register to attend. We do the legwork of providing a venue for the course.

Can I teach?

You don't have to be a trained teacher. If you're smart and passionate about something, we believe you can be a good teacher. Our only criteria is that you teach something interesting!

What happens during a "session"?

Sessions are like highly interactive university tutorials. The key to a fun session is to strike a balance between theory & practice. You want the session to be both fun & edifying. Instructors are there to explain the new concepts, set the exercises, and help students get unstuck.

Money?

A two hour session is typically £20. Most courses last four sessions (£80). Dactic keeps 8% and the rest is paid to the instructor. An instructor might make ~£700.

We've found that for our instructors, the money is an afterthought. They're teaching because they enjoy it.

What's a typical instructor like?

Our instructors are hackers who are expert in a particular subject. They're developers by day, but are excited by the prospect of teaching.

How long do courses last?

Sessions usually occur in the evenings, and weekends. The length of a course is entirely at the instructors discretion. Our courses typically are 8 hours long, in total.

What can I teach?

Our only criteria is that you teach something interesting. Create a compelling course plan, and think about how you can teach it in an engaging manner.

I'd like to teach, but you don't have a course in my area of expertise.

All of the courses you see listed were developed by the teacher with help from us. If you're talented, enthusiastic and knowledgeable, we'd like to help you design a course. Please send us an email.

Who attends these courses?

Our students are usually curious professional developers that are interested in exploring new technologies.
